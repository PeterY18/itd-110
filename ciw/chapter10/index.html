Exercises

  • Exercise 1
  • Portability: Mobile websites are more portable than mobile apps
  • Device Capabiliites: Apps have more capabilities than web browsers
  • Support and upgrading: Apps are more difficult to support and update than websites
  • User experience: apps have full control of user interface. Websites are limited. Both depend on the design.
  • Performance: Apps have a high performance, while webdesign performance will depend on mobile site design and bandwidth speeds
  • Offline browsing: Apps require internet, while HTML5 on browsers can slightly support it
  • Hyperlinks: Apps can link to different areas within and outside the app. Websites can also link to other websites
  • Discoverability: Apps require a strategy to succeed. Websites are found through search engines
  • Distribution and market size: Apps are at the mercy of appstores. Apps require users to dowload which is a barrier to entry. Market size is limited to the number of users on the released platform. Websites can be accessed by anyone
  • Costs: some app stores charge extra for publishing, hosting, and certifiying
  • Some app stores charge extra fees for publishing, hosting or certifiying an app. websites have no costs other than development and hosting
  • Revenue: apps sales must share sales revenue with the app stores after other costs. Revenue is gained after development, hosting, and advertising costs.

Responsive Web Design

  • adapts to screen sizes so that reading and navigation is easy on all devices
  • Responsive and mobile design will optimize a site for smaller screens, simplify navigation, and create faster load times
  • Rendering: RD states client does comutations for resizing and orientation, MD is where server does optimization
  • Coding: RD has One set of HTML and one URL will work on all devices, MD requires a separate domain and is limited to which client devices that can use it.
  • Searches/SEO: RD, use the same domain so links boost the sites search rankings. MD, uses a different domain, so links do not boost the search ranking
  • User Experience: Content that lives on one site with one URL is easier to interact with, share and link to. MD, content on a separate mobile site, which may diminishor duplicate some aspcets of use
  • Maintenance: RD, content that lives on one site with one URL is easier to update and maintain. MD, Content is on a separate mobile site, which requires separate processes to update and maintain
  • Forward compatibility: RD, more forward-compatibility. Programming may require few or not changes to adapt to future devices and browsers. MD, Next-generation mobile devices and browsers ay require a mobile site to be reworked in the future.
