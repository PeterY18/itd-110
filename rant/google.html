Stop Using Google

First off, yes you can call me a hypocrite. I still use google despite everything I'm about to say. A quick synopsis for this post is

  1. Privacy vs. Convenience
  2. Control of the Internet
  3. Censorship and Influence
  4. The Solution

Privacy vs. Convenience

Privacy Check

Privacy these days is at risk due to mass public surveillance. Mass public surveillance can be defined as companies or government collecting information on a large group of common people. 99% of these common people are not criminals or connected to anything dangerous. However, in the name of "security" governments and companies around the world spy on their innocent civilians. Google is one of these companies

Convenience

After learning all of this privacy knowledge and my delve into free and open source software I have decided to take a more balanced approach to all of this. After all, the services Google provides are extremely convenient. With 15 gigabytes of free storage, access to the entire productivity suite, and cross platform capabilities many people have good reason to use Google products. However, there are many problems associated with Google that all contribute to the balancing act of privacy vs. convenience.

Control of the Internet

I would like to start off by saying one of my pet peeves is when people call whatever company they dislike a "monopoly." Merriam-Webster define monopoly as "exclusive ownership through legal privilege, command of supply, or concerted action." It is easy to call Google a monopoly but is not. Google's browser just happens to be more than 50% of all browsers. This could be a problem.

Chrome is a Good Browser

Chrome is fast, reliable, and integrates perfectly with all of the free software Google provides. From the technology illiterate to the power user, chrome is a decent browser. Google also has their search engine which is the default search engine for a vast majority of the world. Google is everywhere and essential to a working internet. However, the more powerful it gets, the more censorship problems there are. Google is gaining a powerful pivot in the internet.

Censorship and Influence

The trade offs

With so many people relying on Google for search engine queries and access to the world wide web, Google can start controlling what the mass public sees. Google can influence how people think by changing their search engine results. Additionally, Google is notorious for selling users information to advertisers for targeted ads. Google could easily change what ads they want to display to influence a user. Google can continue to do this because it is too convenient for a normal user to change.

The Solution

The simple solution to Google's overwhelming control is to use other alternatives. Firefox is a great browser to use instead of Chromium based ones. If you need a chromium based browser try Brave. DuckDuckGo and Startpage are great search engine alternatives. Cryptpad is a great productivity suite replacement. Switching to an alternative seems overwhelming but just changing slowly to less reliance on Google is a good thing.
